How Hobart House Painters actually works.

The crew on a Hobart house painting job is the same crew that quoted it — Trent (the principal, 32 years on the tools), plus two-to-four hands who have been with the business between three and nine years. We don't sub work out to a chasing-cheap labour-hire mob, we don't have a roster of itinerant fly-in workers, and we don't put apprentices on a job without a registered tradesperson actively supervising. The continuity is the work.

The Hobart side of it.

Hobart is not a market we read about; it's the market we live in. Trent is a CFA captain at the local fire station — the kind of detail that doesn't fit on a quote but does explain why the crew is on time, why the price doesn't drift, and why we'd rather pass a job to a mate than do work in a suburb we don't know. Every quote we issue is from someone you can put a face to.

How we operate

What makes a Hobart House Painters job different.

One person, start to finish.

Trent quotes the job and runs the crew that does the job. There is no handover, no junior estimator who lowballs the quote then upcharges on the day, and no third-party project manager who hasn't been on a job site in five years. If you ring at 7pm on a Wednesday, you get the person who's going to be on your property on Monday morning.

Written, fixed-price quotes.

Every quote we issue is all-inclusive — site assessment, weather risk, access risk, permit work if relevant. No "from $X" range pricing, no day-of-build surprise variations, no mid-job upsell. If a surprise lands on the dig that we did not allow for, it's our cost, not yours.

A crew that stays the same.

The Hobart crew is the same five people it has been for four years. We don't sub work out to chase-cheap labour-hire crews, we don't put apprentices on a job without a registered tradesperson actively supervising, and we don't run a roster of fly-in workers. Continuity is what makes the work hold up at the 10-year mark.
